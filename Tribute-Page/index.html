Sarojini Naidu

The Nightingale of India

Here's a time line of Sarojini Naidu's life:

  • 1879: Sarojini Naidu was born on February 13th in Hyderabad, India, to Aghornath Chattopadhyaya and Barada Sundari Devi.
  • 1895: At the age of 16, Sarojini Naidu married Dr. Govindarajulu Naidu, a non-Brahmin and a doctor by profession.
  • 1905: She joined the Indian National Congress and became an active participant in India's freedom struggle.
  • 1915: She was appointed as the President of the Indian National Congress Women's Section.
  • 1917: She played a crucial role in the Champaran Satyagraha movement led by Mahatma Gandhi.
  • 1924: She became the first woman Governor of an Indian state, serving as the Governor of Uttar Pradesh until 1929.
  • 1929: She was elected as the President of the Indian National Congress, becoming the first Indian woman to hold this position.
  • 1930: She participated in the Salt Satyagraha movement, led by Mahatma Gandhi, and was imprisoned for several months.
  • 1931: She represented India at the Round Table Conference in London.
  • 1947: She participated in the Indian Independence movement and witnessed India's independence from British rule on August 15th.
  • 1949: She passed away on March 2nd at the age of 70 in Lucknow, India.
